Buyer's Checklist

Six things to check
on any premium PVC board.

Short answer: judge the cap, the core, the surface, the warranty, the fastening, and the origin. Everything else is marketing.

1. Cap chemistry and coverage

The cap faces the sun, so its chemistry drives color and gloss retention. ASA (acrylonitrile styrene acrylate) is the chemistry specified for long-term exterior weathering. Confirm the board is capped, and ideally capped on all four sides so every edge is protected.

Look for: a weatherable ASA cap, four-sided coverage.

2. Core construction

A quality cellular PVC core is engineered for stiffness, screw-hold, and moisture resistance, with consistent cell density across every color. No wood fiber means no moisture path at cut ends or fastener holes.

Look for: cellular PVC, consistent density, no wood fiber.

3. Surface: laminated vs solid color

A laminated wood-grain surface gives the most realistic look, with variegated grain that reads across the whole board. A solid-color board with a subtle texture gives a cleaner, classic finish that ages uniformly. Both are valid; pick for the look you want.

Look for: laminated grain for realism, or solid color for classic simplicity.

4. Warranty terms, read in full

Compare both the fade and the structural coverage, residential and commercial, and note how fade is measured (Delta E) and whether coverage is full or prorated over time.

Look for: clear fade and structural terms you can actually read.

5. Fastening options

The best lines support both top-fastening and hidden clips. Grooved profiles that use a standard groove geometry work with hidden fastener systems you may already stock.

Look for: top-fasten and hidden-clip ready, standard groove geometry.

6. Where and how it's made

A board extruded, capped, and finished on one domestic line has more consistent chemistry from core to cap. American Pro's boards are made by Patwin Plastics in Linden, NJ, with 50 years of extrusion experience.

Look for: single-line manufacturing and a real extrusion track record.
