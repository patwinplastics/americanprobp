Built for New York Weather

A porch that survives
a New York winter.

Lake-effect snow out west, deep Adirondack cold up north, and humid summers downstate, all of it punishes a wood porch. New York is one of the hardest climates in the country on porch flooring, and it's where cellular PVC earns its keep.

01

No freeze-thaw rot

Cellular PVC has no wood fiber, so meltwater has no path in. It won't absorb moisture, swell, cup, or rot through the dozens of freeze-thaw cycles a New York winter throws at a porch.

02

Slip-resistant on ice melt

A slip-resistant surface that stays safer than wood when there's snow underfoot, salt residue, or morning dew, right through the long New York shoulder seasons.

03

Won't grey out or splinter

Pressure-treated wood greys and splinters under New York weather. American Pro holds its wood-grain finish with a 25-year stain and fade limited warranty, no sanding, staining, or sealing.

04

Made right next door

American Pro is extruded in Linden, NJ, minutes from the New York line. That means short lead times and American-made boards for porches statewide, from NYC to the North Country.

Does PVC porch flooring hold up to New York winters?

Cellular PVC is one of the best porch flooring choices for New York's climate. From lake-effect snow in the west to deep Adirondack cold and humid downstate summers, New York porches face heavy moisture and dozens of freeze-thaw cycles a year. American Pro cellular PVC has no wood fiber to absorb moisture, so it won't rot, cup, or swell, and its slip-resistant surface stays safer than wood under snow and ice melt. It carries a 25-year stain and fade limited warranty.

Is American Pro porch flooring made in the USA?

Yes. Every American Pro porch board is extruded by Patwin Plastics at its vertically integrated facility in Linden, New Jersey, just outside New York. American Pro porch flooring is made in the USA and has supplied New York porches with American-made cellular PVC since 2001.
