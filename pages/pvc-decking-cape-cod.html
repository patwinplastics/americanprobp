Built for the Coast

A deck that survives
a Cape Cod summer.

Salt spray, southwest sun, and thirty-knot wind off the water are exactly what destroys a wood deck. Cape Cod is one of the hardest coastal climates on the East Coast for exterior decking, and it's where cellular PVC earns its keep.

01

Salt air won't rot it

Cellular PVC has no wood fiber, so salt-laden air has nothing to break down. Legacy PVC won't rot, swell, or cup through Cape Cod summers or long, damp shoulder seasons.

02

UV-stable in the sun

South-facing Cape decks take direct sun for hours. Legacy PVC is UV-stabilized and won't fade more than 5 Delta E under sunlight and weathering, with color that's solid through the board.

03

Won't grey out or splinter

Mahogany, cedar, and pressure-treated pine all grey and splinter under coastal weather. American Pro holds its embossed wood-grain finish with no sanding, staining, or sealing.

04

Stocked close to the job

No barge from off-Cape. Stonewood Products stocks American Pro Legacy PVC decking at both the Harwich flagship and the Mashpee store, plus national online shipping for off-Cape contractors.

Does PVC decking hold up to Cape Cod salt air?

Cellular PVC is one of the best decking materials for coastal Cape Cod. It has no wood fiber to rot, no wood pores for salt to work into, and no ferrous components in the board itself to corrode. American Pro Legacy PVC decking carries a 25-year residential warranty against splitting, flaking, rotting, and structural defects, and against fade beyond 5 Delta E.

What's the best decking material for a Cape Cod home?

For coastal Cape Cod homes, cellular PVC decking is a top pick because salt air, sun, and moisture cycle through the boards for months at a time. American Pro Legacy PVC decking is solid-color cellular PVC with an embossed wood-grain texture, a UV-stabilized formulation, and a 25-year residential warranty, engineered for exactly this environment.
