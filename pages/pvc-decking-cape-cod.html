Built for the Coast

A deck that survives
a Cape Cod summer.

Salt spray, southwest sun, and thirty-knot wind off the water are exactly what destroys a wood deck. Cape Cod is one of the hardest coastal climates on the East Coast for exterior decking, and it's where cellular PVC earns its keep.

01

Salt air won't rot it

Cellular PVC has no wood fiber, so salt-laden air has nothing to break down. Legacy PVC won't rot, swell, or cup through Cape Cod summers or long, damp shoulder seasons.

02

UV-stable in the sun

South-facing Cape decks take direct sun for hours. Legacy PVC is UV-stabilized and won't fade more than 5 Delta E under sunlight and weathering, with color that's solid through the board.

03

Won't grey out or splinter

Mahogany, cedar, and pressure-treated pine all grey and splinter under coastal weather. American Pro holds its embossed wood-grain finish with no sanding, staining, or sealing.

04

Stocked close to the job

The Cape Cod salt-air question

Why PVC over composite
on Cape Cod.

Every Cape Cod homeowner comparing decking asks the same question: PVC or composite? Here is the honest technical breakdown, unfiltered, because on the Cape the answer really does come down to salt air.

Cellular PVC (Legacy PVC)

  • Zero wood fiber. The board is solid PVC through the core. Salt air has nothing organic to attack.
  • No moisture uptake. Water beads and runs off. Cape Cod winter freeze-thaw does not swell or crack the board.
  • Mold and mildew resistant. Nothing for spores to feed on, even in shaded, damp yards near dune grass or under tree canopy.
  • Consistent color through the board. Cut ends and scratches match the surface, so weathered edges do not show a different color underneath.
  • 25-year residential warranty against splitting, flaking, rotting, and structural defects, plus fade beyond 5 Delta E.
  • Solid, quiet underfoot. Higher density than composite, so heels do not knock and the deck does not sound hollow.

Capped composite

  • Wood fiber core, plastic cap. The cap protects the surface, but if the cap is breached at a cut end or fastener, salt-air moisture can reach the core.
  • Can absorb some moisture. Uncapped cut ends are the weakness. On Cape Cod, cut ends face salt spray for decades.
  • Mold on the surface can appear in damp, shaded yards, especially in wooded Cape lots with poor airflow.
  • Cap-only color. Scratches and cuts reveal the darker core, requiring careful edge treatment on stairs and rim boards.
  • Warranties vary by tier. Some cover 25 or 30 years, some 10, depending on line.
  • Value pick for inland or partly sheltered decks. Great option for homes back from the water where salt spray does not hit the deck directly.

The honest take: if your Cape Cod deck is within a mile of open water, exposed to salt spray, or sits in a shaded coastal yard, cellular PVC is the safer long-term pick. Composite is a fine choice for inland Cape properties where the deck sits back from the shoreline. Ordering Legacy PVC
on Cape Cod.

A short, honest walkthrough of what to expect from first phone call to boards on your Cape Cod job site. Whether you are the homeowner or the builder, this is the flow.

  1. 01

    Get free hand samples.

    Order free Driftwood and Khaki samples from American Pro directly, or stop by Stonewood in Harwich or Mashpee to see full boards in person. Cape Cod cedar-shingle homes read differently from Osterville white-clapboard homes, so seeing the color against your siding matters.

  2. 02

    Measure and price the job.

    Your builder or Stonewood's team can convert your deck plan into a board count. Legacy PVC comes in 12, 16, and 20 ft lengths. Standard board is 1 in by 5.5 in actual, in solid or grooved profile for hidden-fastener installs.

  3. 03

    Pull permits.

    Attached and elevated decks in Barnstable County require permits under Massachusetts 780 CMR (10th Edition, effective January 1, 2025). Rail height, stair geometry, load paths, ledger attachment, and flame-spread ratings all fall under code review. Requirements vary by Cape town, so check with your local building department in Barnstable, Falmouth, Chatham, Yarmouth, or wherever the deck lives.

  4. 04

    Order the boards.

    Call Stonewood Harwich at 508-430-5020 or Mashpee at 508-477-9950. They stock Driftwood and Khaki in inventory. National online orders go through 1-855-786-9663.

  5. 05

    Take delivery on the Cape.

    Stonewood delivers across Cape Cod and the South Shore, and ferries to Martha's Vineyard and Nantucket. Winter delivery windows may vary. Ask about pallet drop, unload, and site-access requirements when scheduling.

  6. 06

    Install with proper hardware.

    Cape Cod's salt air is hard on fasteners. Use stainless-steel screws or the InvisiClip hidden fastener system designed for grooved PVC decking. Keep metals consistent to reduce galvanic corrosion. Follow board-gap and joist-spacing recommendations for coastal installs.

Can Legacy PVC decking handle a Cape Cod winter?

Yes. Cellular PVC has near-zero moisture uptake, so freeze-thaw cycles do not swell, crack, or split the board the way they attack wood-fiber decking. Snow, ice, and calcium chloride ice melt do not damage Legacy PVC. Clear snow with a plastic-edged shovel, never a metal one, to protect the surface finish.
