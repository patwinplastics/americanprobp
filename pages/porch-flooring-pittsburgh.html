Snow, river-valley humidity, road salt, and dozens of freeze-thaw cycles a year are exactly what destroys a wood porch. Western Pennsylvania is one of the harder climates in the country on porch flooring, and it's where cellular PVC earns its keep.

01

Cellular PVC has no wood fiber, so meltwater has no path in. It won't absorb moisture, swell, cup, or rot through repeated Pittsburgh freeze-thaw cycles.

Does PVC porch flooring hold up to Pittsburgh winters?

Yes. Cellular PVC is one of the best porch flooring choices for Pittsburgh's freeze-thaw climate. It has no wood fiber to absorb moisture, so it won't rot, cup, or swell through Western Pennsylvania's repeated freezing and thawing, and its slip-resistant surface stays safer than wood under snow, ice melt, and morning dew. American Pro porch flooring carries a 25-year stain and fade limited warranty.

Why does PVC porch flooring last longer than wood in Western PA?

Pittsburgh and Western Pennsylvania porches face heavy snowfall, river-valley humidity, and dozens of freeze-thaw cycles each winter, which is exactly what rots and splinters wood and greys out pressure-treated lumber. American Pro cellular PVC has no exposed wood fiber, so water has no path in. It won't rot, splinter, or grey out, and it never needs sanding, staining, or sealing.
